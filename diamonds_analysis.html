2. Explore relationship among varibles¶

Cut vs Price¶

Diamond Cut specifically refers to the quality of a diamonds angles, proportions, symmetrical facets, brilliance, fire, scintillation and finishing details. These factors directly impact a diamonds ability to sparkle, along with its overall aesthetic appeal.

The GIA grades Diamond Cut on the scale of Ideal, Excellent, Very Good, Good, Fair and Poor. The Ideal and Excellent grades, depending on Diamond Shape, signify proportions and angles cut for maximum brilliance and fire.
Source: https://www.diamonds.pro/education/4cs-diamonds/

Polish vs Price¶

Polish refers to the degree of smoothness of each facet of a diamond as measured by a gemologist.

EX: Excellent. No polish defects visible at 10x magnification.
VG: Very Good. Any defects are extremely difficult to see at 10x magnification.
G: Good. Any defects are difficult to see at 10x magnification.

Symmetry vs Price¶

As the name implies, diamond symmetry simply refers to how symmetrical a diamonds facets and angles are. For diamonds with a symmetry grade of Excellent to Good, symmetry should not be used as a primary factor in choosing a diamond, since each of these grades is possible in diamonds of exceptional appearance.

Carat Weight vs Price:¶

One carat equals 200 milligrams or 1/5 of a gram in weight. For diamonds under one carat, each carat is divided into 100 points similar to pennies in a dollar. 0.75 ct. = 75 points, 1/2 ct. = 50 points.

3. Tell story¶

The key to a diamonds value is its rarity, and no two diamonds are alike. Rarity is determined by a diamonds unique characteristics as measured by the Four Cs: Cut, Color, Clarity and Carat Weight. Thus, our story is mainly based on the relationship between the Cut, Clarity, Carat Weight and Diamonds Price.

Part 1: Carat Weight and Price¶

In [14]:
plt.figure(figsize= (10,6))
plt.plot(df['Carat Weight'],df['Price'],'b.')
plt.title('Diamonds Carat Weight vs Price',fontsize=15)
plt.xlabel('Carat Weight',fontsize=12)
plt.ylabel('Avg Price',fontsize=12);

Analysis:

  • From previous analysis, the Carat Weight has highly positive relationship with Price -- 0.86. As we can see, there is a clear bound line in the scatter plot showing the specific relationship between entire carat weight range and the correspoding minimum prices.

  • Also, we find that as carat weight increases, the range of possible prices also increases. We think that this may be caused by more flexibility and modification of diamond making process. For example, a heavier diamond means a bigger volumn. This is convenient for improving beauty, quality and clarity of the diamonds, like by precisely cutting and modifying, compared to the smaller ones.

  • FL(Flawless) only exists in Ideal and Signature-Ideal, which means the higher Cut quality is, the higher Clarity grade and the higher prices of diamonds will be.
  • For Signature-Ideal grade of Cut, we find that VVS1 has lower average prices than VVS2, which is strange. We think the factor can be Symmetry, because Symmetry is more important in diamonds of VVS2 Clarity and higher.
  • For Fair grade of Cut, there are no clear price rules to follow.
